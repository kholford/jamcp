Appendix B: Trust Design & Intent

1. Purpose

This document outlines what the trust is intended to accomplish. It is not a legal draft, nor is it meant to dictate the structure of the trust; rather, it conveys provisions desired to be included within the trust document.

2. Core Objectives

  • Centralize control of assets
  • Enable execution of the care plan
  • Avoid probate
  • Maintain clarity and enforceability

3. Key Functional Requirements

3.1 Asset Control

  • All major assets coordinated under the trust and related estate plan:
  • Home
  • Automobile
  • Brokerage account
  • Checking / cash accounts
  • Traditional IRA and related beneficiary designations
  • Other financial accounts

3.2 Home Handling

  • Home is intended to be sold following the transition period specified in the Care Plan
  • Timing should balance:
  • safe transition of co-resident
  • orderly preparation for sale
  • value optimization
  • prompt conversion to liquid care-funding assets
  • No life estate structure

3.3 Use of Funds

  • Assets used for care expenses
  • No gifting that would affect Medicaid eligibility

3.4 Partner Consideration

  • Short-term transition period (no long-term housing provision)
  • Owner partial support of home expenses during transition period
  • Optional inheritance provision (if desired)

3.5 Children Distribution

  • Any remaining assets distributed to children
  • Equal or defined allocation

3.6 Medicaid Alignment

  • Structure supports compliant spend-down and coordinated asset liquidation
  • Avoids disqualifying transfers

3.7 Trustee Authority

Trustees should have authority to:

  • Sell property
  • Manage financial accounts
  • Coordinate retirement account distributions
  • Make tax-aware withdrawal decisions for care funding
  • Pay for care
  • Make necessary financial decisions

4. Funeral Trust Coordination

  • Separate irrevocable trust may be established

  • Designed to:

  • cover funeral expenses

  • reduce countable assets for Title 19 qualification

5. Timing Considerations

  • Trust should be established before Grantor’s transition to assisted living
  • Assets transferred promptly
  • Beneficiary designations reviewed and aligned with the overall plan

6. Key Principle

The trust should:

  • support the plan
  • simplify execution
  • avoid ambiguity
  • coordinate efficiently with the financial care-funding strategy

Conclusion

The trust is a critical component of the plan, providing the structure needed to carry it out effectively and consistently. It is intended to support orderly asset management, continuity of care funding, and long-term execution of the overall care plan.
